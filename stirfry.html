Chicken Stir-Fry with Vegetables

Instructions:

Prepare the Chicken: Slice chicken breasts into strips.

Cook the Chicken: Heat a bit of oil in a large pan or wok over medium heat. Add the chicken strips and cook until fully cooked and lightly browned, about 5-7 minutes. Remove the chicken from the pan and set aside.

Cook the Vegetables: In the same pan, add a little more oil if needed. Add mixed vegetables (broccoli, bell peppers, snap peas, carrots) and stir-fry until tender-crisp, about 4-5 minutes.

Make the Sauce: In a small bowl, mix together low-sodium soy sauce and a bit of grated ginger to taste.

Combine: Return the cooked chicken to the pan with the vegetables. Pour the sauce over the chicken and vegetables, stirring to coat evenly. Cook for an additional 2-3 minutes.

Serve: Serve the stir-fry over cooked brown rice.

Benefits:

Lean Protein: Chicken breast is a high-quality source of lean protein, important for muscle health and repair.

Nutrient-Rich Vegetables: Mixed vegetables provide vitamins, minerals, and antioxidants that support overall health and reduce inflammation.

Healthy Carbohydrates: Brown rice provides complex carbohydrates and fiber, which help with sustained energy and digestion.

Low Sodium: Using low-sodium soy sauce reduces sodium intake, which is beneficial for maintaining healthy blood pressure levels.

Anti-Inflammatory: Ginger in the sauce has anti-inflammatory properties that can help with digestion and overall well-being.
