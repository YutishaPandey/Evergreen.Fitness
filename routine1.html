Routine 1: Exercises to Prevent Arthritis

This routine includes exercises designed to enhance joint mobility and muscle strength, aimed at preventing and managing arthritis symptoms.

Exercise 1: Seated Leg Lifts

Sit upright in a chair with your feet flat on the floor. Slowly lift one leg straight out in front of you, hold for a few seconds, and then lower it back down. Repeat with the other leg.

45

Take a 20-second break before starting the next exercise.

20

Exercise 2: Seated Side Leg Raises

Sit upright in a chair with your feet flat on the floor. Lift one leg out to the side as high as you comfortably can, hold for a few seconds, and then lower it back down. Repeat with the other leg.

45

Take a 20-second break before starting the next exercise.

20

Exercise 3: Walking on the Spot

Stand upright and march in place, lifting your knees as high as you comfortably can. Swing your arms naturally as you march.

45

Take a 20-second break before starting the next exercise.

20
