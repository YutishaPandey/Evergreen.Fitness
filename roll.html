Vegetable Soup with Whole Grain Roll

Instructions:

Prepare the Vegetables: Chop 2-3 carrots, 2-3 celery stalks, 2 tomatoes, and a handful of fresh spinach.

Cook the Soup: In a large pot, heat a little oil over medium heat. Add the chopped carrots, celery, and tomatoes. Cook for about 5 minutes until the vegetables begin to soften.

Add the Broth: Pour in 8 cups of low-sodium broth (see instructions below for making it). Add 1 bay leaf and, optionally, a few sprigs of thyme or rosemary.

Simmer: Bring the mixture to a boil. Reduce the heat to low and let it simmer for 15-20 minutes, or until the vegetables are tender.

Add Spinach: Stir in the chopped spinach and cook for an additional 2-3 minutes until wilted.

Season: Taste and adjust seasoning with salt and pepper as needed. Be cautious with salt if using low-sodium broth.

Serve: Ladle the soup into bowls.

Prepare the Whole Grain Roll: If store-bought, warm the roll according to package instructions. If homemade, bake or toast the roll as desired.

Enjoy: Serve the soup hot with the whole grain roll on the side.

How to Make Low-Sodium Broth:

Ingredients:

1 large onion, chopped

2-3 cloves garlic, minced

2-3 carrots, chopped

2-3 celery stalks, chopped

8 cups water

1 bay leaf

A few sprigs of thyme or rosemary (optional)

Salt to taste (use sparingly or omit for low sodium)

Instructions:

In a large pot, heat a little oil over medium heat. Add onion, garlic, carrots, and celery and cook for about 5 minutes until softened. Add water, bay leaf, and thyme or rosemary if using. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer for 30-40 minutes. Strain the broth through a fine-mesh sieve to remove the vegetables and herbs. Let the broth cool, and then refrigerate. Skim off any fat that rises to the top before using.

Benefits:

Nutrient-Dense: The soup is rich in vitamins, minerals, and antioxidants from a variety of vegetables.

Hydrating: The broth helps keep you hydrated and supports overall health.

Fiber-Rich: Vegetables and whole grain rolls provide fiber, which supports digestive health and helps you feel full.

Low Sodium: Using low-sodium broth helps manage blood pressure and supports heart health.

Comforting and Filling: The soup is warm, comforting, and filling, making it a great option for a nutritious and satisfying meal.
